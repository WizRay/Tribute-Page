Tim Berners-Lee

The inventor of World Wide Web

Timeline of the Man behind WWW

  • 1955 - born in London, England
  • 1969 - Attended Emanuel School
  • 1973 - Studied Bachelor of Arts in Physics at The Queen's College, Oxford
  • 1976 - worked as an engineer at the telecommunications company Plessey
  • 1978 - joined D. G. Nash, where he helped create typesetting software for printers
  • June to December 1980 - worked as an independent contractor at CERN
  • late 1980 - worked at John Poole's Image Computer Systems, Ltd
  • 1984 - returned to CERN as a fellow
  • 1989 - proposed a project based on hypertext to facilitate sharing and updating information among researchers
  • 1990 - published the first web site
  • 1991 - posted a public invitation for collaboration with WorldWideWeb project
  • 1994 - founded W3C, comprising of companies willing to create standards and recommendations to improve the quality of the Web
  • 2001 - became a patron of the East Dorset Heritage Trust
  • 2001 - elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS)
  • 2004 - accepted a chair in computer science at the School of Electronics and Computer Science, University of Southampton, Hampshire, to work on the Semantic Web (Web 3.0)
  • 2004 - knighted by Queen Elizabeth
  • 2004 - elected as a member into the American Philosophical Society
  • 2007 - appointed to the Order of Merit
  • 2007 - elected as a member into the National Academy of Engineering
  • 2009 - would work with the UK government to help data more open and accessible on the Web
  • 2009 - launched the World Wide Web Foundation (WWWF) in order to campaign to "advance the Web to empower humanity by launching transformative programs that build local capacity to leverage the Web as a medium for positive change".
  • 2013 - awarded the inaugural Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering
  • 2017 - received the 2016 ACM Turing Award

" We could say we want the Web to reflect a vision of the world where everything is done democratically. To do that, we get computers to talk with each other in such a way as to promote that ideal. "

-- Tim Berners-Lee
